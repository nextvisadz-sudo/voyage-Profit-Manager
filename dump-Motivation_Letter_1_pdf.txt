Dear Members of the Committee,
My name is Ghofrane Mekhalledi, a Telecommunications graduate from University of
Batna 2, Algeria. I am writing with great enthusiasm to express my motivation to join the
Master’s Degree in Telecommunications and Internet Technologies –
Telecommunications Systems at Politecnico di Bari. With a solid academic background,
a strong command of English, and a determined goal to deepen my expertise in modern
telecommunication systems, I am convinced that this program is the right path to achieving
my academic and professional ambitions.
During my Bachelor’s Degree in Telecommunications (2022–2025), I focused on subjects
such as signal processing, transmission systems, telecommunication protocols,
embedded systems, and network design. These topics allowed me to understand how data
flows through modern infrastructures and how to optimize these systems for speed, security,
and scalability. I also practiced technical documentation, client-focused communication,
and cross-functional team collaboration, all of which are essential for a future career in
telecommunications engineering.
My passion for cutting-edge communication technologies, especially in signal
transmission and network optimization, has grown steadily throughout my academic
journey. I am eager to further specialize in areas covered by your program, particularly in
advanced telecommunications systems, Internet of Things (IoT), and network
infrastructure design. The Telecommunications Systems option at Politecnico di Bari
stands out due to its deep technical focus and hands-on approach, aligning perfectly with
both my educational foundation and career goals.
Politecnico di Bari is a university I admire not only for its academic reputation in
engineering but also for its commitment to research, innovation, and internationalization.
Studying in an environment that fosters excellence and collaboration will allow me to
sharpen my technical skills while being part of a global community. I am especially excited
about opportunities to engage with professors and peers working on applied research in
telecommunications.
